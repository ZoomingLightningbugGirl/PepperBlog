Capsicum pubescens


About Pubescens

The Capsicum pubescens species is extremely small, typically ranging from 1 to 3 inches (2.5 to 7.5 cm). They have a fruity flavor mixed with hints of smokiness. They're native to Bolivia, Peru, and Chile. They are packed with nutritious dietary fiber and antioxidants such as capsaicin.


History

The Capsicum pubescens originated in the Andes, but is native to Bolivia, Pero and Chile. The first peppers of the species likely produced petite fruits that were swallowed by birds and spread across the region. They are one of the most uncommonly used pepper varieties.
